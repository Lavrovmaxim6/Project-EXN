How it works
1
Silent Deployment
Audit-AI installs silently through your existing SSO or IT management tools. Employees never see it. There's no opt-in, no popup, no friction — it's just on.
2
Real-Time Detection
The extension monitors AI tool usage across the browser — past, present, and future. Every interaction is captured: employee identity, machine ID, AI platform used, content type, file size, and timestamp.
3
Instant Intelligence
Instead of dumping raw logs onto your security team, Audit-AI classifies every event automatically. Each entry arrives pre-interpreted: what kind of data was shared, how severe the risk is, and what action to take.
Your security team shouldn't have to investigate 2,000 events a day.
Audit-AI does it for them.
Policies
Data Retention Policy
Audit-AI stores only what's necessary to identify risk. Logs are capped and never leave the organization's local environment.
  • Maximum of 500 events stored at any time — oldest entries roll off automatically
  • All data is stored locally in the browser via chrome.storage — nothing is sent to external servers
  • Logs can be cleared at any time by an administrator from the dashboard
  • No personally identifiable content is retained — only metadata about what was shared
Privacy Policy
Monitoring data stays within your organization. Audit-AI does not transmit, sell, or share any employee activity data with third parties.
  • Employee identity is captured via Chrome profile or local machine hostname — no additional tracking
  • No content of messages or documents is stored — only signals (size, type, platform, risk level)
  • Data is accessible only to administrators with access to the Audit-AI dashboard
  • Compliant with GDPR, CCPA, and SOC 2 monitoring disclosure frameworks
Acceptable Use Policy
Employees are expected to follow their organization's guidelines around AI tool usage. Audit-AI enforces those guidelines in real time.
  • Approved AI tools are defined by IT or security administrators — all others are monitored
  • Sharing confidential data (client records, financials, source code, PII) with AI tools is prohibited unless explicitly authorized
  • Block Mode can be enabled to prevent sensitive content from reaching AI platforms entirely
  • Repeated policy violations escalate automatically to High risk classification
Monitoring Disclosure Policy
Organizations deploying Audit-AI are responsible for disclosing monitoring to their employees in accordance with local employment and privacy law.
  • Employees should be notified at onboarding that AI tool usage on company devices is monitored
  • Disclosure language should be included in employee handbooks and IT acceptable use agreements
  • In jurisdictions requiring explicit consent (e.g., EU, CA), written acknowledgement is recommended
  • Audit-AI provides monitoring capability — legal compliance is the responsibility of the deploying organization
